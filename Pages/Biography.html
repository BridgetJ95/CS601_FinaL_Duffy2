Biographical facts: Bridget Duffy

The early years

I grew up in a suburban town named Cranford in New Jersey. I lived in the same house there my whole childhood. Now, I live in Somerville; I've come full circle! I was born while my dad was in grad school at Harvard; my mom had me at Beth Isreal Hospital, right across the street from Simmons University where I recently got my MFA, and took my home to their apartment in Somerville--the very same zip code I live in now. But since they moved to NJ when I was just eigth months old, I don't think I can say that I'm from Somerville.

The later years: education

I went to St. Michael School from kindergarten through eigth grade; Mother Seton Regional High School for high school; University of Pittsburgh for College; and Simmons University for graduate school. I really loved school once I got to college; I've always been fairly good at academics, and once I was in college, I could drill down and focus on learning about my passions. I double-majored in English Fiction Writing and Classical Civilization, completed a certificate in Children's Literature, and got a minor in Latin.

I loved learning Latin. The whole enterprise of translating Latin to English felt like a puzzle to me--figuring out the correct translation of each word, where it fit into the sentence, and how that affected the sentence structure as a whole was so much fun, and so satisfying to watch the pieces click into place.
Writing code scratches a similar itch in my brain.

After undergrad, I went write into grad school, pursuing an MFA in Writing for Children. I'm really proud of the work I did there; it was fantastic to learn and grow as a writer surrounded by people as passionate about children's literature and media as I am.

Present Day

Now, I spend my days working at Cengage Group as a Product Assistant and my Wednesday nights pursing a graduate certificate in Web Application Development at BU Met. I'm interested in shifting my career path toward something involving coding.

However, there's plenty more I like to do with my time! Explore the site to learn more about my interests and passions.
